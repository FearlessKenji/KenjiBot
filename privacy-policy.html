Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 11, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how KenjiBot collects, uses, stores, and deletes information when you add or use the KenjiBot Discord application.

Who We Are

KenjiBot is a Discord bot used for Twitch and Kick live notifications, birthday reminders, reaction roles, and related Discord server utilities. In this policy, "KenjiBot," "we," "us," and "our" refer to KenjiBot.

Information We Collect

KenjiBot only collects information needed to provide its bot features. Depending on which features are used in a Discord server, this may include:

  • Discord server IDs, channel IDs, role IDs, message IDs, and user IDs.
  • Server configuration chosen by server administrators, such as notification channels, ping roles, birthday posting settings, time zones, and reaction-role panel settings.
  • Streamer configuration, such as Twitch or Kick channel names, notification preferences, and optional Discord invite URLs supplied by an administrator.
  • Birthday information that a user chooses to save, limited to Discord user ID, Discord server ID, birth month, and birth day. KenjiBot does not ask for or store birth year.
  • Reaction-role panel content created or converted by administrators, such as panel titles, descriptions, role labels, role IDs, emoji choices, image URLs, thumbnail URLs, channel IDs, and message IDs.
  • Operational logs, such as command errors, startup events, server join events, deleted panel cleanup events, and crash reports. Logs may include Discord IDs, server names, owner usernames, error messages, and stack traces when needed for troubleshooting.

KenjiBot uses Discord's APIs to receive interactions, reactions, and other events needed for the bot to function. KenjiBot has the Message Content intent enabled. Message content is used for features that require it, such as converting an administrator-selected message into a reaction-role panel. KenjiBot does not use message content to build user profiles or to train machine learning or AI models.

Information We Do Not Collect

KenjiBot does not ask for or intentionally collect passwords, Discord login tokens, payment card details, government ID numbers, precise location, health information, or other sensitive personal information.

How We Use Information

KenjiBot uses collected information to:

  • Send Twitch and Kick live notifications to configured Discord channels.
  • Store and apply server notification settings.
  • Store and post birthday reminders when users choose to use the birthday feature.
  • Create, edit, convert, maintain, and clean up reaction-role panels.
  • Add or remove Discord roles when users interact with reaction-role panels.
  • Respond to slash commands, context menu commands, buttons, select menus, and reactions.
  • Troubleshoot errors, protect the bot, and maintain reliability.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce applicable terms and policies.

How We Share Information

KenjiBot does not sell personal information. KenjiBot does not share Discord API data with data brokers, advertising networks, or marketing services.

KenjiBot may share or disclose information only in the following limited situations:

  • With Discord, as necessary for the bot to operate on Discord.
  • With Twitch or Kick APIs, when checking configured streamer channels and posting live notification information. KenjiBot does not send Discord user birthday records to Twitch or Kick.
  • When information is posted into a Discord server as part of a feature, such as a live notification, birthday post, or reaction-role panel.
  • If required by law, legal process, or a valid request from a public authority.
  • To protect KenjiBot, Discord users, server communities, or others from abuse, security threats, or legal liability.

Data Storage and Retention

KenjiBot stores configuration and feature data in a local SQLite database used by the bot. KenjiBot keeps this data only for as long as needed to provide the configured feature, troubleshoot problems, comply with legal obligations, or protect the bot and its users.

  • Server notification settings are retained until changed, removed, or no longer needed.
  • Streamer entries are retained until an administrator removes them or they are no longer needed.
  • Birthday entries are retained until the user removes them, a server administrator disables the feature, or the data is no longer needed.
  • Reaction-role panel data is retained while the panel is active and may be deleted or disabled when the related Discord message or channel is deleted.
  • Raw logs and structured logs are archived after about 1 day and archived logs are deleted after about 30 days, unless a longer period is needed for security, troubleshooting, or legal reasons.

Your Choices and Deletion Requests

You can remove some data directly through KenjiBot commands. For example, you can use the birthday remove command to delete your stored birthday for a server. Server administrators can remove configured streamers, update notification settings, and manage reaction-role panels.

You may also request access to, correction of, or deletion of personal information associated with your Discord account by contacting us at the email address below. We may need enough information to verify the Discord user ID and server involved. Some information may be retained if required for security, legal compliance, dispute resolution, backups, or legitimate operation of the service.

Children's Privacy

KenjiBot is not directed to children under 13 or to anyone below the minimum age required to use Discord in their country. If we learn that KenjiBot has collected personal information from a child in a way that violates applicable law or Discord's terms, we will delete that information where required.

KenjiBot does not sell personal information and does not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. KenjiBot also does not knowingly sell or share personal information of users under 16.

If you are a California resident and want to make a privacy request, contact us using the email address below. We will review and respond to requests as required by applicable law.

Security

We use reasonable administrative and technical measures to protect information processed by KenjiBot. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission over the internet is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.
